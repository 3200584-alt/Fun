About Birds

Birds are warm-blooded vertebrates with feathers, beaks, and lightweight skeletons. They occupy diverse habitats and exhibit a wide range of behaviors, diets, and adaptations for flight, swimming, or ground living.

  • Diet: Ranges from seeds and nectar to insects and fish.
  • Movement: Many birds fly, some swim, and others are flightless.
  • Communication: Songs and calls are important for mating and territory defense.
